This web application lets you dive into real UK crime data using the official data.police.uk API, specifically the “Crimes at Location” endpoint. Instead of giving you a vague overview of crimes within some radius, this tool returns crimes that occurred directly at a pinpointed location. You just need to input four simple details: latitude, longitude, the year, and the month. Once submitted, the app will fetch crime logs from that exact location for the timeframe you selected. The results are laid out clearly and include the category of the crime (like burglary, anti-social behavior, or violence), the specific location, the outcome status (such as “under investigation” or “no further action”), and the date the incident occurred. This helps users, developers, researchers, or just curious folks gain quick, targeted insights about crime activity in any particular area of England, Wales, or Northern Ireland.It’s important to note that while this tool can search anywhere across the UK, data in Scotland is limited. That’s because only the British Transport Police provide data for Scottish regions, so results might appear suspiciously low or even empty, but that doesn't necessarily mean the area is crime-free. It just means the dataset doesn’t fully cover Scottish police forces. So if you're letting users query locations in Scotland, this limitation should be made clear upfront.
